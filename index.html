How Due Score Works ▾
Z-scores each hitter's current drought against his own rhythm, then nudges it by how he's actually hitting the ball lately.
  • The drought. Every hitter's current AB count since his last HR is compared to his own season-long gap pattern, not a flat number — a 30-AB drought means something very different for a guy who usually homers every 12 ABs than one who homers every 28. We z-score it: how many standard deviations beyond his typical gap he is right now.
  • The weighting. That z-score is weighted up for proven power (more season HRs = more signal worth trusting) and for confidence (more historical gaps logged = a sturdier personal average, so one early-season fluke doesn't swing things).
  • The contact nudge. Up to ±15% is layered on top based on Statcast's own per-batted-ball contact-quality grade (1-6, weak contact to barrel — already a joint read on exit velo and launch angle), averaged across every batted ball since his last HR vs. before it, and blended with his season rate so one lucky or unlucky swing in a short stretch can't swing the result on its own. Cooling contact nudges his score down even if the raw AB math says he's "due"; contact that's still scalding nudges it up. Exit velo, Sweet Spot%, and Barrel% are shown below for context but don't separately drive the score — they're already folded into that one grade.
  • Eligibility. z ≥ 1.0, 5+ HR this season, AB/HR ≤ 30, and excludes anyone inactive 5+ days (injured, benched, or demoted) — a stale drought doesn't count. A day-to-day guy who's still in the lineup stays fully eligible; his drought is real, he's just playing through something.
  • Who's shown. By default the list hides players who can't bat today — on the IL, team not playing, or out of a posted lineup — since a due hitter who can't play isn't actionable. Their droughts keep tracking in the background; flip off the "Hide Off / Bench / IL" filter to see them. Day-to-day guys (hurt but not IL'd, 🤕, pulled from ESPN's injury report) are handled by lineup: in today's lineup, he plays through it and the tag drops; out of it, he's treated like a benched/injured guy — hidden by default and dropped from the rankings, but tagged 🤕 (not "bench") so the reason is clear. A separate "Hide Day-to-Day" toggle hides them even when the off/bench/IL filter is off.
💤 Off — no game today 🪑 Bench — not in today's starting lineup 🤕 Day-to-Day — hurt, not on the IL 🚑 Injured — on the IL

How Pick Score Works ▾
Today's best HR matchups, combining recent contact quality, platoon edge, pitch-type overlap, pitcher homer rate, and park.
  • Base power. Starts from each batter's season-long HR rate (HR per AB) — raw power is the foundation, matchup factors adjust it up or down. Thin AB samples are regressed toward league-average HR rate: 4 homers in 51 at-bats looks like an elite rate a full season rarely sustains, so it's pulled back until the sample grows. Low-HR hitters still make the board, they just can't top it on a two-week hot streak alone.
  • Recent contact quality. Same Statcast 1-6 contact-quality grade used by the Due tool, but time-windowed (last 15 game dates vs his own season baseline) rather than drought-anchored — reflects how he's actually squaring the ball up right now.
  • Platoon edge. How this specific batter performs against today's pitcher's handedness vs the opposite side, AND how this specific pitcher fares against batters of this batter's effective side — both shrunk heavily toward league-average platoon splits, since single-season HR-rate splits are too thin to trust at face value.
  • Pitch-type synergy. Cross-references which pitch types this batter has actually gone deep on this season against the pitcher's mix — specifically the mix he throws to this batter's side, not his overall usage. Pitchers attack lefties and righties differently (across the league's top arms roughly a quarter of the arsenal reshuffles by batter hand — e.g. Cristopher Sánchez throws righties mostly changeups but pounds lefties with sinkers), so a lefty slugger is scored against the pitches he'll really see. A batter who homers mostly on fastballs facing a pitcher who throws his side 50% four-seamers is a more interesting target than one whose HR profile doesn't match at all. The mix shown in the pick modal is tagged vs LHB/RHB when a real handedness split is used (it falls back to the overall mix for a side he's barely faced). The edge is regressed by HR sample size, though — 3 of a hitter's 4 homers coming on fastballs says far less than 30 of 40, so a thin profile is pulled toward neutral until it's built on enough homers to trust (flagged in the pick modal when it applies).
  • Park. The game's venue HR/game rate vs league average. Same signal shown on the Schedule tab, baked directly into the score.
  • Weather. Game-time air density (temperature, humidity, pressure, altitude) plus wind projected onto the park's home-plate→center-field axis — hot, thin air and a wind blowing out both help the ball carry; cold, dense air and a wind blowing in suppress it. Roofed parks are treated neutral. The stadium's orientation, roof type, and coordinates come straight from the MLB API.
  • Lineups. Uses confirmed lineups when posted. Before lineups drop, falls back to projected starters — players with 15+ games this season, 1.5+ AB/game, active in the last 7 days — the same pool the books use pre-lineup. Projected picks are labeled so you know which ones are speculative.
  • Openers. When today's "starter" is really an opener (2+ starts averaging ≤3 IP, on a team that's recently used the piggyback pattern), his weight drops to the inning or two he actually covers, and the likely bulk arm — the rested rotation-type pitcher whose turn it is — gets his own platoon and pitch-mix slice of the score. Flagged in the pick modal so you know who you're really betting against.
  • Trimming the board. The full board ships (score ≥ 7) so tracking stays consistent, but a July 2026 backtest against sportsbook closing prices found the edge concentrates at the top: picks scoring 9+ from hitters with ~15+ HR hit 26-34% in-sample, while the long tail (low scores, low-power bats) was longshot-priced and lost money. The Min HR and Min Score filters above cut to that slice — your choices are remembered between visits.

🎯 What Longshots Are ▾
Fringe-power bats (under 10 HR) in a genuinely great spot today — ranked by matchup with recent contact weighted double. High-payout darts, not value.
  • Who's here. Genuinely low-HR bats — the ones the book hangs at long odds (+500 and up). The Picks board leans on raw power, so these guys rarely make it; this is where they surface. Defaults to under 10 season HR; loosen or tighten with the filter.
  • How they're ranked. Same matchup engine as Picks (pitch-type synergy vs the arm's handed mix, platoon edge, park, weather), but recent contact quality is weighted double — a fringe bat squaring the ball up right now is a live dart, a cold one gets buried. Unknown recent form takes a small penalty; longshots live and die on it.
  • Price discipline. Only bet these at a true longshot price — if the book isn't paying better than +600, pass. A fringe bat goes deep rarely enough that anything shorter doesn't cover the misses. Take the big number or leave it; the Results tab shows how they actually land.

Returning Boppers ▾
Injured power bats and when they're due back — the first game off the injury is a classic homer spot.
  • Who's here. Power bats on an injured list or day-to-day per ESPN's injury report — anyone with at least one homer this season, so pitchers and no-power scrubs are filtered out. Each shows his season homers and today's Due / Pick badges where they apply. Tracked returns (the Results tab) are held to a higher bar — 5+ HR — so the return-day homer rate reflects real boppers.
  • Return dates. ESPN's estimated return date — a target, not a promise. Near-term dates are usually solid; a date weeks out can slide with setbacks or rehab stints.
  • Just back. When a hitter drops off the injury report (and isn't on the IL), he's flagged here for his first 3 days back — the "homers in his first game back" window. A guy still on the report but confirmed in today's lineup counts as back too.
  • Results. Every flagged return is tracked: homer within that 3-day window and he moves to the Results tab as a hit; the window closes with no homer and he's logged as a miss. The tab shows the running return-day homer rate.
